The Night We Watched

A projection and sound installation showing a nighttime cityscape through glowing apartment windows. Each window reveals quiet moments of daily life, reflecting the unseen stories that connect us all.

Description

The Night We Watched is a projection and sound installation that captures the quiet pulse of the city at night. Through illuminated windows and layered soundscapes, it reveals fleeting glimpses of human life, turning private spaces into a collective dream.

Concept

Inspired by the rhythm of city nights, this work explores how isolation and connection coexist in urban living. Each window represents a fragment of life—a story happening in parallel to countless others. The installation invites viewers to slow down and imagine what emotions and routines unfold behind the light.

Design Process

I divided the creation into two main stages: capturing footage of apartment windows and designing the projected layout. I composited the scenes using After Effects for layered animations and used Premiere Pro to sync ambient soundscapes and jazz-inspired tones. The sound design blends city ambience, TV static, laughter, and subtle reverb to create a cinematic nighttime texture.

Reflection

This project taught me how storytelling can exist through stillness and rhythm rather than dialogue. By merging projection, sound, and architecture, I learned to build an environment that feels alive and empathetic. It reminded me that even in a city of millions, moments of solitude can connect us in unexpected ways.
